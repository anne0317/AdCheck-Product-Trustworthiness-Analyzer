A few years in and a screw came loose
I had this for 2 or 3 years before it stopped vibrating with any intensity. Kind of happened over night. I looked up this issue (knowing that I was well past my warranty) and found that an internal screw (there's literally just one) has a common tendency to come loose. It seems like this should be a very easy fix - open the shell of the toothbrush and tighten said screw. Well, it appears that Sonicare designed their toothbrush to not be user-friendly, in the repair field. First you have to have a vice grip or locking pliers to squeeze the base of the toothbrush until you have a small gap to pry the bottom off. This process feels like you're going to break everything. Then you have to forcefully jam the toothbrush end against something sturdy until it pops outout of the casing. This also feels like you will break a lot of things. I thought I could get inside part out with some small pliers - instead I broke the copper coil holder (a very brittle plastic) that is necessary for the device to have any charging function. I fixed the small issue with the screw - but this toothbrush is officially on its last charge - less I want to do some delicate soldering work. This would be a 5-star product. However it is clear to me that it is designed to fail and when it fails it is designed to not be easy to repair - even though the most common fail-point is simply one screw - and that one screw is something that doesn't even need to be a screw. The screw could have been replaced with something more secure that wouldn't eventually wiggle loose. The screw could have been glued in place so that it couldn't come loose... many things could have fixed this design flaw. However, I am convinced that this company wants their products to break and not be repaired.
